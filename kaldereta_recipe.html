Kaldereta - A Filipino Classic

Kaldereta is a hearty and flavorful Filipino stew made with tender meat, vegetables, and a rich tomato-based sauce. Traditionally made with beef, this dish is perfect for family gatherings or special occasions.

Ingredients:

  • 1 kg beef (or pork), cut into cubes
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 large tomatoes, chopped
  • 1 red bell pepper, chopped
  • 1 green bell pepper, chopped
  • 1 cup potatoes, peeled and sliced
  • 1/2 cup carrots, peeled and sliced
  • 1 cup tomato sauce
  • 1/2 cup liver spread (optional for richness)
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 1/4 cup beef broth or water
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1/2 tsp ground black pepper
  • 1/4 tsp chili flakes (optional for spice)
  • 1/4 cup green peas (optional)
  • 1/4 cup raisins (optional for sweetness)
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1/4 cup olives (optional for flavor)

Instructions:

  1. Heat oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add the beef and sear until browned on all sides. Remove from the pot and set aside.
  2. In the same pot, add onions and garlic. Sauté until soft and fragrant.
  3. Add the chopped tomatoes and bell peppers. Cook for a few minutes until the tomatoes soften.
  4. Return the beef to the pot and stir to combine with the vegetables.
  5. Pour in the tomato sauce, soy sauce, and beef broth. Stir and bring to a simmer.
  6. Season with salt, pepper, and bay leaf. Cover and let it cook for about 1.5 to 2 hours, or until the beef is tender.
  7. Add the potatoes, carrots, peas, and raisins (if using). Continue to cook for an additional 15-20 minutes, or until the vegetables are tender.
  8. Stir in liver spread and olives, and cook for another 5 minutes for a creamy consistency and added depth of flavor.
  9. Taste and adjust seasoning as needed. Serve hot with steamed rice.

Helpful Tips:

  • For a richer flavor, use beef stew meat or short ribs.
  • If you don't have liver spread, you can substitute with pâté or leave it out for a lighter version.
  • Kaldereta is often served with a side of steamed white rice, but you can also pair it with bread to soak up the sauce.
  • For a spicier kick, you can add a few slices of chili or use chili flakes to taste.
