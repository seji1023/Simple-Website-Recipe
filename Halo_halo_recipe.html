Halo-Halo - The Ultimate Filipino Refreshment

Halo-Halo is a fun and refreshing Filipino dessert made of a variety of colorful ingredients, topped with shaved ice and sweet milk. Perfect for hot summer days or whenever you're in the mood for a cool, sweet treat.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup shaved ice
  • 1/4 cup sweetened red beans (e.g., munggo or adzuki beans)
  • 1/4 cup sweetened jackfruit, sliced
  • 1/4 cup sweetened banana (saba variety), sliced
  • 1/4 cup sweetened coconut strings (macapuno)
  • 1/4 cup leche flan, cubed
  • 1/4 cup purple yam (ube halaya)
  • 1/4 cup nata de coco (coconut gel)
  • 1/4 cup sweetened gulaman (jelly cubes)
  • 1/4 cup corn kernels (optional)
  • 1 scoop vanilla or ube ice cream
  • Sweetened condensed milk (for drizzling)
  • Evaporated milk (for drizzling)

Instructions:

  1. Prepare all the ingredients by cutting and sweetening the fruits and beans ahead of time. Drain excess syrup from the sweetened ingredients if necessary.
  2. In a tall glass or bowl, place a layer of shaved ice at the bottom.
  3. Layer each of the ingredients, starting with the sweetened beans, jackfruit, banana, coconut strings, and ube. Add the leche flan, nata de coco, gulaman, and corn kernels (if using).
  4. Top with a scoop of ice cream (vanilla or ube works best) and a generous drizzle of sweetened condensed milk and evaporated milk.
  5. Mix everything together before eating to enjoy the full combination of textures and flavors. Serve immediately with a straw and a spoon!

Helpful Tips:

  • You can customize your Halo-Halo by adding or removing ingredients based on your preferences. Some variations include adding sweetened pineapple, toasted rice flakes, or other tropical fruits.
  • For a creamier treat, you can substitute some of the milk with coconut milk.
  • If you're making Halo-Halo for a group, serve in individual cups or bowls for easy mixing and sharing.
  • If you prefer a lighter version, you can reduce the amount of sweetened condensed milk or opt for a sugar-free variant.
